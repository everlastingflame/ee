[[Continuous Distributions]]
The exponential distribution describes the amount of time $X$ until an event with rate $\lambda$ occurs. 
$$
\begin{align}
f(x) = \lambda e^{-\lambda x}, \, \text{for} \, x \in [0, \infty) \\
\mu = \frac{1}{\lambda}, \, \sigma^2 = \frac{1}{\lambda^2} \\
m(t) = \left(\frac{1-t}{\lambda} \right)^{-1}
\end{align}
$$
